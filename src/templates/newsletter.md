# Newsletter Template

Use this template for Inclusive Tech Club newsletter drafts.

---

## Title

[Short, direct, and specific. Not a teaser — a statement. "Why accessibility audits fail disabled users" not "Accessibility: are we doing it right?"]

---

## Opening Thesis

[2–3 sentences. The central argument. Should give the reader real value even if they stop here. Do not start with a question. Start with a statement.]

---

## The Problem

[Name the specific, real problem. Not the symptom — the system. Who created it? What maintains it? What does it cost?

2–4 short paragraphs. Concrete, not abstract. Use a real example where possible, or mark [EXAMPLE NEEDED].]

---

## Why It Matters

[Connect to stakes. Business, human, or both.

What happens if this doesn't change? Who pays that cost?

2–3 paragraphs. Specific.]

---

## Inclusive Design Lens

[The analysis that is uniquely Inclusive Tech Club.

Name the power dynamics. Surface the intersectional dimension. Challenge the default framing.

2–4 paragraphs. This is where you go further than the LinkedIn post could.]

---

## Business Value Translation

[Translate the inclusive design insight into business language.

Revenue, risk, cost, brand — pick the ones that apply. Give product leaders and founders the language to use in their next meeting.

2–3 paragraphs.]

---

## Action Step

[One thing the reader can do this week. Specific. Starts with a verb.

Not "think about this more." Give them an action, a question to ask, or an experiment to run.

1–2 paragraphs.]

---

## CTA

[One clear ask. Download, workshop, consultation, reply. Not three asks.]

## Newsletter Voice Checklist

- [ ] No banned phrases (run voice checker)
- [ ] Opening thesis delivers value in the first 3 sentences
- [ ] At least one specific example (or [EXAMPLE NEEDED] flag)
- [ ] Power dynamics named, not implied
- [ ] Business value section is concrete, not aspirational
- [ ] Action step starts with a verb and is specific
- [ ] Heading hierarchy is logical (H2 > H3) for screen readers
- [ ] Plain language throughout — readability grade 8–10
- [ ] One CTA only
