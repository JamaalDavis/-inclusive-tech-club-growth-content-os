# Brand Guide — Inclusive Tech Club

## Brand Position

Inclusive Tech Club is the platform that makes inclusion operational.

Not aspirational. Not decorative. Operational.

## Brand Promise

If you engage with Inclusive Tech Club content, frameworks, or workshops, you will leave with something you can use — a framework, a better argument, a clearer problem statement, or a sharper set of questions to bring back to your team.

## Brand Personality

| Trait | What It Means |
|-------|---------------|
| Rigorous | We do not make claims we cannot support |
| Practical | We give people things they can use |
| Honest | We name uncomfortable things clearly |
| Accessible | We write at a level that serves practitioners, not just academics |
| Grounded | We connect theory to real products and real systems |
| Independent | We are not beholden to corporate talking points about DEI |

## What Inclusive Tech Club Is Not

- Not a corporate DEI program
- Not a compliance checklist vendor
- Not a motivational speaking circuit
- Not an accessibility auditing firm (though we connect to that work)
- Not a generic "design thinking" consultancy
- Not a newsletter that reposts LinkedIn trends with a "thoughts?" caption

## Audience Language

Talk to the audience like they are smart people trying to do better work under real constraints.

Do not talk to them like:
- They need to be converted to the cause
- They are villains for not knowing this already
- They are incapable of handling complexity
- Their only obstacle is awareness

## Content That Earns Trust

Trust comes from:
1. Naming the real problem, not a polite approximation of it
2. Being right about the business stakes
3. Offering frameworks that hold up under scrutiny
4. Not pretending inclusion is easy when it isn't
5. Treating disabled people and marginalized communities as full stakeholders, not use cases
