# Working Rules

These rules govern every output this system produces. They are not suggestions.

---

## Rule 1: No Publish-Ready Content Without Human Review

Every generated asset has a status of `drafted` until Jamaal explicitly approves it. The system does not mark content `approved` or `published`. That is a human action.

**Why:** Automated publishing under your name is how you lose your voice, your credibility, and your audience's trust simultaneously.

---

## Rule 2: Preserve Jamaal's Voice

Every draft must pass the voice checker before being marked `reviewed`. If it uses banned phrases, generic AI language, or hollow corporate framing, it goes back for revision.

Voice is not a style preference. It is the foundation of trust with the audience.

---

## Rule 3: Connect Inclusion to Business Value

Every piece of content must demonstrate why inclusion matters in terms the audience can act on. The moral case is real and important, but it is not enough on its own in most professional contexts.

If a draft only makes the moral case without touching the business case, flag it for revision.

---

## Rule 4: Flag Claims That Need Citations

Do not invent statistics. Do not make up research findings. When a draft makes a specific factual claim (a number, a study result, a named phenomenon), flag it with `[CITATION NEEDED]` until it is sourced or removed.

---

## Rule 5: Inclusion Review Is Not Optional

Every generated content asset must pass through the inclusion reviewer before being marked `reviewed`. The reviewer checks for:
- Shallow inclusion language (performative, not operational)
- Missing power dynamics analysis
- Accessibility gaps
- Harmful framing
- Overconfident AI claims

A `block` status from the inclusion reviewer means the content does not move forward without revision.

---

## Rule 6: Accessibility Notes in All Structured Formats

Every carousel outline must include alt text drafts and visual direction that supports screen readers and high-contrast viewing.

Every video script must include caption guidance and on-screen text notes.

Every newsletter must be structured for screen reader navigation (proper heading hierarchy, plain language).

---

## Rule 7: Repurpose, Don't Repeat

When generating derivative content, the repurposing engine should preserve the original content pillar, business value framing, and audience intent. Repurposing is not copying with a different format label. It is adapting an idea to serve the reader in a different context.

---

## Rule 8: Governance Agent Has Veto Power

If the governance agent returns a `block` status on any artifact, that artifact cannot proceed to the next stage. The `block` is not advisory. It is a hard stop.

---

## Rule 9: Do Not Treat Disabled People as Edge Cases

Content that frames accessibility as serving "edge cases," "the 1%," "special needs," or "niche users" must be revised. Disabled people are not a minority interest group to be served after the "main" product is done. They are part of the full audience.

---

## Rule 10: Outputs Folder Is Not the Archive

The `outputs/` folder is for working drafts and exports. It is not a publishing queue. Content must be moved to the appropriate platform by Jamaal, manually, after approval.
