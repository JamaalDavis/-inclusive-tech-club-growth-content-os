# About Jamaal Davis

## Who I Am

Jamaal Davis is an inclusive designer, service designer, content creator, and founder/operator of Inclusive Tech Club.

My work sits at the intersection of inclusive design, accessibility, human-centered AI, power dynamics in product development, and the business case for equitable product strategy.

I am not an "influencer." I am a practitioner who writes about real problems, real systems, and real business consequences of getting design wrong.

## What Inclusive Tech Club Is

Inclusive Tech Club is a platform for inclusive design, accessibility, human-centered AI, and equitable product strategy.

It is built as a place where inclusion becomes operational, not inspirational wallpaper.

Current and planned offerings:
- Content (LinkedIn, newsletter, carousels, short video)
- Frameworks, canvases, and toolkits
- Workshops and training
- Consulting and advisory
- Community and paid membership

## My Core Beliefs

- Accessibility is not compliance. It is revenue protection, risk reduction, and basic human respect.
- AI moves fast. That is not inherently good. Speed without accountability causes harm.
- Every product has politics. When teams call it "business requirements," they are avoiding the conversation, not eliminating it.
- WCAG is the floor, not the ceiling.
- Inclusion is not a sprint. It is a structural commitment.
- The people most harmed by bad design are the people least likely to be in the room when decisions get made.

## My Audience

I write for:
- Product leaders who need to make the business case for accessibility and inclusion
- Inclusive design practitioners who want sharper frameworks and better language
- Founders and operators who want inclusion to be part of growth, not just a footnote
- Accessibility advocates who want to connect compliance to lived experience and systems change
- People who are tired of shallow corporate DEI content and want something with teeth

## What I Am Building Toward

A trusted, sustainable platform that:
1. Generates leads for workshops, consulting, and courses
2. Builds a community of practitioners and leaders who take inclusion seriously
3. Demonstrates that inclusive design is good business — not despite rigor, but because of it
4. Operates from a foundation of equity, not extractive hustle
